Brief Biography

I was born in a small beautiful warm city Wuhu located in the middle east of China. I obtained my B.S. degree in Engineering from Anhui University, Hefei, China, in 2003, my M.S. degree in Computer Science from the Graduate University of Chinese Academy of Sciences, Beijing, China, in 2006, and my Ph.D. degree in Computer Science from Hefei University of Technology, Hefei, China, in 2015, under the supervision by Professor Xindong Wu. After obtained my master's degree, I worked as a senior software engineer in a R&D department at Anhui USTC iFLYTEK Co. Ltd., a Chinese public listed IT company known for its speach and language processing technology, where I spent more than five years in designing and implementing various kinds of systems. I started my Ph.D. study in Sept. 2012, focusing on data mining and machine learning. During my Ph.D. study, I worked as a visiting scholar in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Central Arkansas under the support of China Scholarship Council (Sept. 2013 through Sept. 2014). Currently, I am also a post-doc researcher in my affiliation.

Research Interests

  • data mining, machine learning, and their applications in business, education, finance and other disciplines
  • big data analytics, data engineering and management
  • distributed systems, cloud computing, application layer protocol and network programming

    • Current Project: Machine Learning with Crowdsourcing

    Crowdsourcing provides a convenient solution to obtain annotated data and involves massive human beings in machine learning procedures. However, due to the uncertainty of non-expert workers on Internet, machine learning with the crowdsourced annotated data or with human interference is full of challenges. We are seeking new theories, models, and algorithms to improve the performance of the machine learning in crowdsourcing scenarios. Our research topics include, but not limit to:
  • Inference (probabilistic graph model)
  • Supervised learning (label utilization strategies, ensemble learning, classification, clustering)
  • Active learning (instance selection and labeler selection strategies, time series modeling)
  • Label noise handling (label noise identification and correction)
  • Applications in text and image processing
    • My on going work focuses on Noisy Correction for Crowdsourcing Quality Control (submitted to IEEE Trans. Neural Network and Learning Systems, minor revision), Label Quality Improvement with Instance Features (submitted to IEEE Trans. Systems, Man and Cybernetics: Systems), and Ensemble Learning for Crowdsourcing.
